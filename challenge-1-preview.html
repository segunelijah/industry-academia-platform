All that you need to know about The Annual Business Plan Competition 2023
The Annual B-Plan Competition is organised by Team Aavishkar, NIT Durgapur which will be held during Aarohan (March 09-12, 2023), the annual techno-management fest of NIT Durgapur which is the second-largest fest in eastern India.
A business plan is a formal written document containing business goals, the methods on how these goals can be attained, and the time frame within which these goals need to be achieved. It also describes the nature of the business, background information on the organization, the organization's financial projections, and the strategies it intends to implement to achieve the stated targets. In its entirety, this document serves as a road map that provides direction to the business.
Eligibility Criteria:
The competition is open for teams comprising of undergraduate and postgraduate students.
Rules and Regulations:
The team must submit their B-Plan abstract in the format given by the organizing committee. (It is available below the contact details section on this page.)
Maximum 4 members permitted in a team (plan can be submitted individually as well).
More than 1 team can participate from a college.
One participant cannot be a part of more than one team.
No change of team structure is permitted after the team is registered.
One of the team members should be Registered as Team Leader.
Process:
First teams of min. 1 and max. 4 need to register and submit an abstract of their B-Plan idea according to a given format. Then a certain number of teams will be selected and will proceed to the onsite round to be held at NIT Durgapur.
Format:
This is a two-phase competition.
Phase I is the online round where teams need to submit their abstract of B-Plan in pdf format. Teams selected from Phase I will get a chance to proceed to Phase II.
Phase II is an onsite round where selected teams from Phase I will get a chance to present their B-Plan in front of a panel of judges at NIT Durgapur. It will be conducted in NIT Durgapur, West Bengal.
Judging Criteria:
Inclusiveness: 30
Simplicity/ Implementation/Applicability: 20
Originality: 20
Multidisciplinary: 20
Clarity in presentation:10
Total score - 100
